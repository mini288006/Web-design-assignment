Freaquently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How long will I have to take it?

A pill is usually absorbed into the blood through the stomach walls after it is swallowed – these can become active in a few minutes but usually take an hour or two to reach the highest concentration in the blood. IV drugs are injected directly into the blood work much faster – sometimes in seconds or minutes.
Why Are Pharmacists Important?

Within the healthcare system, pharmacists are the medicines experts. Their valuable knowledge of how medicines work and how they should be used make them vital members of health care teams.
How long does it take for my medical supply to reach?

Usually we first contact the nearest pharmacy near you and we try to get a response from them. After that we will get the product from them and deliver them to you as soon as possible
Why are some products expensive than usual?

The reason why some products are more expensive than the original is because we included the price for delivery and some pharmacy sell the products more expensive than the other ones but we try to pick the cheapest price of them all.
